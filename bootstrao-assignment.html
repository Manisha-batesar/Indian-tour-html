Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, born in a modest family in Rameswaram, India, was an eminent scientist, engineer, and visionary leader. He played a pivotal role in India’s space and missile development programs and earned the moniker “Missile Man.” His work at ISRO and DRDO contributed significantly to India’s scientific prowess. In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. During his presidency, he continued to inspire the nation with his vision for a developed India. Dr. Kalam was not only a scientist but also a prolific author, with books like “Wings of Fire” and “Ignited Minds” motivating countless individuals, particularly young students, to dream big and work hard. His dedication to education and youth empowerment led to the creation of the “Kalam-Raju Tablet,” aimed at providing quality education to rural students. Though he passed away in 2015, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam’s legacy as a role model and a symbol of knowledge, humility, and dedication to the betterment of society continues to influence and inspire people.
